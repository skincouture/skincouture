Non-Surgical Thread Lift

  1. Home
  2. Non-Surgical Thread Lift

A non-surgical thread lift is a minimally invasive cosmetic procedure that uses dissolvable sutures to lift and tighten sagging skin on the face and neck. The procedure involves inserting small, barbed threads into the skin, which are then gently pulled to lift and reposition the skin.

Non-surgical thread lifts are most commonly used to treat sagging skin in the mid-face, jowls, and neck. The procedure can also be used to lift the eyebrows and smooth out wrinkles and lines in the forehead and around the eyes. The procedure is relatively quick, usually taking around 30-60 minutes to complete. Patients may experience some mild discomfort during the procedure, but no anesthesia is required.
